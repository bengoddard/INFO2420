Web Site Critiques

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Pitchfork

My first impression of this site is that this website is set up in a great way for you to be able to see a lot of info right off the bat. They organize the flow of info by having links in the navbar at the top of the page, and some other links on the right side of the page. The site structure makes sense to me because everything is clearly labeled and organized.

I think the site's graphics and multimedia enhance the site because it helps you see or visualize what each article is about. The home page loaded very fast. The text color is and size are great and make it very easy to read. The site does have a navbar and it has basically every link you need in it.

For most of the pages, there is a very similar layout and color scheme. It does a great job changing layout of everything when the window size changes. I think the home page does a really good job of showing the articles and multimedia in an interesting way that keeps you engaged.
